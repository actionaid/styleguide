Numbers

Spell out numbers from one to nine. Use numerals after that unless it’s the start of a sentence.

Numbers with more than three digits take commas:

999

1,000

150,000

Use million or billion for numbers above 999,999. One billion children are living in poverty.

Dates

Spell out days of the week and months in the order day date month year.

Sunday 1 September 2020

Decimals and fractions

Spell out fractions.

Yes: two-thirds

No: 2/3

Use a decimal when a number can’t be easily written out as a fraction, like 1.375 or 47.2.

Percentages

Use the % symbol instead of spelling out "per cent", unless the percentage starts a sentence, in which case spell out the number and per cent.

Ranges and spans

Use a short dash (-) without spaces to indicate a range or span of numbers.

It takes 20-30 days.

Money

When writing about currency, use the symbol before the amount.

£1 million

US$20

¥1

Other than pounds sterling, currency should always be presented with its sterling, euro or US dollar equivalent (depending on your audience) in brackets.

55 rupees (£1)

Don’t abbreviate million and billion and include a space after the numeral.

£5 million, US$12 billion.

Telephone numbers

Use spaces between groups of numbers. Our users are all over the world, and so are we, so always use a country code.

+27 11 731 4500

Temperature

Use the degree symbol and the capital C abbreviation for Celsius.

28°C

Time

Use numerals and am or pm, with no space in between. Use dots not colons between hours and minutes. Don’t use minutes for on-the-hour time. 

7am 

7.30pm

Use a hyphen between times to indicate a time period. 

7am-10.30pm

Spell out international time zones: Nepal Standard Time, Australian Eastern Time. If a time zone does not have a set name, use its Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) offset.

Abbreviate decades when referring to those within the past 100 years:

The 00s

The 90s

When referring to decades more than 100 years ago, be more specific:

The 1900s

The 1890s
