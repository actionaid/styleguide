Opinion

We publish articles that contain expert opinion about international development issues. We start debates, and join existing ones. We aim to engage our audiences and position ActionAid as a place where interesting debate and conversation happens.

We accept contributions from ActionAid staff and others. Opinions are always written by named individuals. Authors need to have a profile.

Opinions can be any length. As with all content, quality trumps quantity. As a rough guide 500-1000 words is usually about right. Longer-form opinions are also fine, but these should be genuinely substantial and in-depth, and not an excuse for waffle.

Do this

Be casual, but smart

This isn’t an academic paper, so there’s no need to be stuffy or overly formal. Share your knowledge while casually engaging your readers with conversational language.

Be specific

If you're writing about data, put the numbers in context. If you're writing about a place, tell people where it is. Don’t assume prior knowledge of your subject.

Show evidence

Identify trends, over time, and make statistics relatable.

Yes: "50 women farmers lose their land every hour in Ghana."

No: "423,000 female farmers lost their land in 2018 in Ghana."

Get to the point

Get to the important stuff straight away, and don’t bury the lead. The inverted pyramid is a useful model.

Make it easy

Articles should be scannable and easy to digest. Break up your text into short paragraphs of three or four sentences, and use subheadings. Look at the headings section for more guidance on how to orientate your readers.

Cut the jargon

This doesn’t mean dumbing down: even complex ideas are best communicated using accessible language. If you really need to use an abbreviation or an acronym, follow the guidelines to minimise confusion and exclusion.

Link it up

Link away from ActionAid if it helps you explain something.

Use pictures

Include images in your articles when it makes sense. Make sure you use alt text.
