Job

Description: Information about a job vacancy.

Strategic aims: Get the best applicants to apply for ActionAid jobs

Digital-first content on the new site means that where possible, content isnâ€™t hidden away in downloadable PDFs. So ideally, all of the information about the job would be written concisely on a single web page, as it makes it less confusing.

But as second preference, the web page is kept fairly short and all the subheadings are filled in with relevant information, including just enough information about the role for the applicant to decide whether they are potentially suitable. Applicants can then download further information as a PDF.

Do this

Be human

Writing in a warm and friendly way will show people that ActionAid is a great place to work. Use the future tense and the second person to create an instant rapport with them.

Be inspiring

Use language that excites people about the role, the aims of ActionAid and how they will help to change the world in their new job.

Get to the point

The more concise and focused your job advert copy is, the higher quality of applications you will receive.

Think about the user experience

Applying for a new job can be a stressful process, plus it might be the first time a new person has interacted with ActionAid. Give people a good impression of your organisation by being well organised and sensitive. Be clear about what you expect from them from the start of the process to the finish.
