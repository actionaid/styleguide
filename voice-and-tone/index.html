Voice and tone

Our voice and tone defines the way we express ourselves through our content.

Voice

Our voice is the organisation’s personality, as expressed through content: on websites, on social media, in emails. It is distinctive and flexible but also identifiable. It is the personification of our relationship with our users. It brings the organisation to life and inspires support.

Our voice is about who we are, not the things we do. It describes our personality, not the attributes of our work.

Our voice is a one-size-fits-all jacket that all staff can wear when creating any kind of content for the organisation.

We are:

  • Thoughtful, but not stuffy or scholarly.
  • Energetic and occasionally irreverent, but never silly.
  • Passionate, radical and brave, but not shouty.
  • Opinionated and bold, but not closed-minded or overbearing.
  • Optimistic, but not unrealistic.
  • Straight-talking, but not aggressive.

Tone

We consider our audience, the context and the state of mind of our audience.

We do not change who we are, but we do moderate our tone, depending on whether we’re talking about an emergency or land rights.

We’re:

  • Humble and down-to-earth
  • Positive and upbeat
  • Human and authentic
  • Sensitive and empathetic
  • Respectful
