Publication

Ideally, short reports and other publications such as briefings should be created digital-first, because digital content is more accessible and more user-friendly than a PDF. This means that instead of producing a PDF, then uploading it to the website for people to download, the publication is created as a web page, or series of webpages, in the first instance.

The new site allows us to create in depth long-form content that loads faster, is mobile-friendly, is more findable by search engines and is updatable.

Most of our existing publications are PDFs. For those we can create engaging landing pages where people can read a summary of the content online, as well as downloading the PDF.

Do this

Pull out the interesting stuff

The content on the page should stand as interesting content in its own right. Start with the issue, not with details about the project or the publication. Downloads add a further level of depth for people who want to explore further.

Think about SEO and your users

Add details about the project where it gives useful, interesting information but don't prioritise this content.

Paddle, swim, dive

Publications are often the deepest level of content, offering the full version of the thinking and research. If the full report is the dive and the swim is the executive summary, what’s the paddle?

Manage expectations, entice

Let people know a little about what they'll get if they download the report. Encourage deeper engagement, but don’t force it.

Give people ways to get involved

Some people will be happy paddling; give those people ways to engage (by donating, for example, or by campaigning).

Like this

  1. Titles should explain what the publication is about.

  2. Subtitles allow some additional context. A good subtitle pulls readers in to the content, encouraging them to read more.

  3. Use the publication date of the publication.

  4. Start with the issue, not with details about the project or the publication. The content on the page should stand as interesting content in its own right. Downloads add a further level of depth for people who want to explore further.

  5. Summarise the problem early on.

  6. Add details about the project where it gives useful, interesting information but don't prioritise this content.

  7. Emphasise the community- and people-powered aspects.

  8. Let people know a little about what they'll get if they download the report.
