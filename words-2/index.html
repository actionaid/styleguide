Words

These words can be slippery. Here’s how we write them. This is a growing list; if you have suggested additions, please let us know.

ActionAid
one word, not two; both As are capital letters

blog
a blog is collection of blog posts; never use blog to mean a single post; as much as possible, use article instead of blog post.

Côte d’Ivoire

not the Ivory Coast

email
never hyphenate; never capitalise, unless it begins a sentence

emoji
singular and plural

the Gambia

hashtag

homepage

internet

never capitalise unless it begins a sentence.

login (noun, adjective), log in (verb)

OK

online

never capitalise unless it begins a sentence.

signup (noun, adjective), sign up (verb)

tweet, retweet

URL

Don’t use

 

liaising with key local stakeholders

Try to talk about people; be as specific as possible

global south, global north (it’s misleading). If it’s unavoidable, don’t capitalise.

beneficiaries

 

third world; first world

 

at the end of the day

 

evidenced

people living in poverty

poor people

Talk about how we can support people living in poverty (and citizens acting in solidarity with them) to become rights activists.

rights holder

young men and women

youth or young people

women's economic rights

women’s decent work
