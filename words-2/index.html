Words

These words can be slippery. Here’s how we write them. This is a growing list; if you have suggested additions, please let us know.

ActionAid
one word, not two; both As are capital letters. As far as possible, try to portray ActionAid as a single, coherent body. So when talking about our work in a particular country from an international perspective, say "ActionAid in Uganda" or something to that effect, rather than "ActionAid Uganda".

blog
a blog is collection of blog posts; never use blog to mean a single post; as much as possible, use article instead of blog post.

Côte d’Ivoire
not the Ivory Coast

email
never hyphenate; never capitalise, unless it begins a sentence

emoji
singular and plural

the Gambia

hashtag

homepage

internet
never capitalise unless it begins a sentence.

login (noun, adjective), log in (verb)

OK

online
never capitalise unless it begins a sentence.

signup (noun, adjective), sign up (verb)

tweet, retweet

URL

Words and phrases to avoid

Jargon, elitism,  anything you’d need a degree to understand.

Don’t use

Try instead

Capacity building

Training (not trainings)

Civil society

Charities, activists

Stakeholders

Governments, communities, people, partners, trustees, staff, supporters (whoever you’re talking about)

Beneficiaries

Affected people

global south, global north (it’s misleading, and oversimplified). If it’s unavoidable, don’t capitalise.

Communities, people

Poor people

People living in poverty

Third world, first world

Developing countries, developed countries

Evidenced by

As seen in... shown by...according to

Rights holder

Talk about how we can support people living in poverty (and citizens acting in solidarity with them) to become rights activists and hold governments and corporations to account.

Duty bearer

Decision maker, government, corporation,

Women’s economic empowerment

women's economic rights

Youth

young men and women

Foregrounding

Promoting, emphasising, championing

Neoliberal macroeconomic orthodoxy

Societies / corporations driven by profit and greed.

Cost cutting measures which erode workers’ rights.

Dialoguing

Discussed with, feedback from

Actors

Charities, UN, be specific

Gender responsive public services / GRPS

Services like nurseries, care homes or water points near to the village …

...services which make women’s lives easier and enable them to focus on earning money.

Arenas

Opportunities to hold decision-makers accountable

NFIs, Non food items

Essential supplies such as xxxx

Multi-sectoral

In coordination, collaboration with ...
