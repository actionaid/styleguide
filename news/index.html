News

News stories are brief, factual, timely accounts of ActionAid activity or things that that are relevant to ActionAid’s work. News stories are written and edited for the website audience, not for journalists, though they may include notes for journalists.

News stories are brief, factual, timely accounts of ActionAid activity or things that that are relevant to ActionAid’s work. News stories are written and edited for the website audience, not for journalists, though they may include notes for journalists.

They’re designed to keep people informed and in the loop, and encourage sharing.

News stories don’t have a named author: they are written by ActionAid. They are usually 200-400 words long.

Do this

Be precise and accurate

Give enough context

News stories should make sense on their own, without being overloaded with background. Provide links in case people want to find out more.

Get to the point

Get to the important stuff straight away, and don’t bury the lede. The inverted pyramid is a useful model.

Make it easy

New stories should be scannable and easy to digest. Break up your text into short paragraphs of three or four sentences.

Cut the jargon

This doesn’t mean dumbing down: even complex ideas are best communicated using accessible language. If you really need to use an abbreviation or an acronym, follow the guidelines to minimise confusion and exclusion.

Use pictures

Include images in your articles when it makes sense. Make sure you use alt text.

Like this

  1. Titles should be short, interesting and descriptive. Don’t use “ActionAid” in headlines.
  2. Use the inverse pyramid model, starting with the most interesting content rather than the background.
  3. Keep quotes idiomatic. They should sound like someone talking.
  4. Add background further down the story.
  5. Include links so that people can explore further.
