Campaigns

Our campaigns are about speaking truth to power and holding organisations and people to account using evidence-based research. We create landing pages for all ActionAid campaigns to engage, empower and provide useful materials.

Campaign pages group relevant news, stories and opinions; case studies; and publications.

Do this

Rock the boat

We don’t shy away from making values-based judgements about situations that are unacceptable. Describe the reality, using hard-hitting language where appropriate to communicate the consequences of injustices. Don’t tell people what to feel; let the words evoke the emotions. This inspires people to act.

Focus your message

Keep text on your campaign page short and snappy, and signpost to places people can go to find out more information about the issue.

Stay positive

We’re brave and outspoken, we build movements. After diagnosing the problem, stay positive about the solution and provide leadership on how to get there.

Give people ways to get involved

We’re creating activists, not parrots. Give people a variety of actions they can take to to get involved, from paddling (by example signing a petition or tweeting) to diving (like attending an action or organising an event)

Like this

  1. Keep campaign titles short.
  2. Introduce the problem that the campaign will solve early on.
  3. Give the campaign some context to help people frame it.
  4. Tell people what needs to change.
  5. What ActionAid is doing is important, but shouldn’t be the first thing users come to.
  6. Link to the evidence.
  7. Provide a way for people to get involved.
